World War I was sparked by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand and ended with the collapse of four empires. It was primarily a war of attrition defined by static trench warfare. World War I caused roughly 40 million total casualties, the vast majority being active combatants. World War II was caused primarily by the unstable peace left by WWI and was characterized by rapid, highly mobile warfare known as Blitzkrieg. WWII was the deadliest conflict in human history, resulting in an estimated 70 to 85 million deaths, with civilians making up the majority of the casualties. WWII ended with the dropping of two atomic bombs on Japan and led to the creation of the United Nations.
